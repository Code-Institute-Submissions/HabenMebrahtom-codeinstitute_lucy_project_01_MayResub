The world's most famous early human ancestor, the 3.2-millionyear-old ape "Lucy" was the first Australopithecus afarensis skeleton ever found in Ethiopia and discovered in 1974 by paleontologist Donald C. Johanson. Australopithecus afarensis was the earliest known human ancestor species for about 20 years.
